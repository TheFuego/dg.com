Saint Sava Temple

The Temple of Saint Sava is a Serbian Orthodox church which sits on the Vračar plateau in Belgrade, Serbia and it's the fourth biggest Orthodox church in the world. It was planned as the bishopric seat and main cathedral of the Serbian Orthodox Church. The church is dedicated to Saint Sava, the founder of the Serbian Orthodox Church and an important figure in medieval Serbia. It is built on the presumed location of St. Sava's grave. It's one of the greatest symbols of Belgrade and is also closelly associated with Partizan as it is close to it's stadium and is visible from the west stand.

Kalemegdan fortress

The Belgrade Fortress consists of the old citadel (Upper and Lower Town) and Kalemegdan Park (Large and Little Kalemegdan) on the confluence of the Sava and Danube rivers, in an urban area of modern Belgrade, Serbia. Located in Belgrade's municipality of Stari Grad, the fortress costitutes the specific historical core of the city and is one of the most important representatives of Belgrade's cultural heritage.

Raven

The common raven (Corvus corax), also known as the western raven or northern raven when discussing the raven at the subspecies level, is a large all-black passerine bird.Because of its black plumage, croaking call, and diet of carrion, the raven is often associated with loss and ill omen, in most cultures, the raven was and still is, considered as the bird of death, which is a fitting symbol for the club who's supporters call themselves the Gravediggers and pride themselves on their black colors and morbid and counterculture symbols.

Pobednik

Pobednik or 'The Victor' is a monument in the Upper Town of the Belgrade Fortress, built to commemorate Serbia's victory over the Ottoman and Austro-Hungarian empires during the Balkan Wars and the First World War. Cast in 1913, erected in 1928, and standing at 14 metres (46 ft) high, it is one of the most famous works of Ivan Meštrović. It is also one of the most visited tourist attractions in Belgrade and one of its most recognizable landmarks.

Symbols of Belgrade

Belgrade, lit. 'White City', is the capital and largest city in Serbia. It is located at the confluence of the Sava and Danube rivers and the crossroads of the Pannonian Plain and the Balkan Peninsula. There are approximately one-thousand sports facilities in Belgrade, many of which are capable of serving all levels of sporting events. It is the home of nearly 1.7 million people and 43 professional and semi-professional football clubs.
